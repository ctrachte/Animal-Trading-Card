Killer Whale

Killer whales feature strongly in the mythologies of indigenous cultures, with their reputation ranging from being the souls of humans to merciless killers.

  • Scientific Name: Orcinus orca
  • Lifespan in the wild: Between 30-70 years
  • Habitat: Any marine environment.
  • Size (length): Male: 6-8m, Female: 5-7m
  • Weight: Male: 6 tonnes, Female: 3 tonnes

Killer whales are apex predators that live in nearly every ocean in a variety of marine environments. They are known to be highly intelligent and social. Killer whales are sophisticated hunters using vocal techniques and strategies much like that of wolves.
